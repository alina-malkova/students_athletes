# Student Athletes: Economic Distance and NCAA International Enrollment

This project investigates how economic distance between countries and the United States shapes the enrollment of international student athletes in NCAA Division I programs. Using institutional-level data from IPEDS and the NCAA membership database, combined with macroeconomic indicators from the World Bank, the analysis examines whether cross-country differences in GDP per capita, cost of living, and purchasing power predict the volume and distribution of foreign athletes across U.S. colleges and universities.

## Data Sources

- **IPEDS** (Integrated Postsecondary Education Data System) — institutional characteristics, enrollment, and financial data for U.S. colleges and universities.
- **NCAA Membership Database** — roster and eligibility information for Division I student athletes, obtained via automated scraping.
- **World Bank Open Data** — GDP per capita, PPP conversion factors, and other macroeconomic indicators by country and year.

## Raw Data

Raw data files are **not included** in this repository because of their size. They are available via the zip archive (`Raw Data *.zip`) in the project's OneDrive folder or through the shared Google Drive link. See the scraping and data-preparation notebooks for details on how the datasets were collected and merged.
